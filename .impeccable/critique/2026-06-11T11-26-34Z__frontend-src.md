#### Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM Assessment:**
The application successfully avoids first-order AI slop (it is not a basic light-mode SaaS copy nor a generic neon-black crypto theme). The dark void canvas (`#0f172a`) tinted with brand neutrals feels premium and solid. However, second-order aesthetic traps exist:
- **Identical Card Grids:** In `Analytics.jsx`, the top row of stat boxes (Total Draws, Hot, Cold, Top Combos) are identical in layout, size, and border. Similarly, in `Tools.jsx`, the quick reference boxes look exactly alike, making the interface feel structured but slightly generic.
- **Banned Typography Characters:** Em-dashes (`—`) are scattered across UI labels in `AIPredict.jsx` (lines 336, 555), `Tools.jsx` (line 265), and `Analytics.jsx` (lines 605, 618), violating the project's absolute ban.

#### Overall Impression
LottoLens presents a highly tailored "Analyst's War Room" feel that fits its goal of displaying mathematical lottery analysis. The dark theme is rich, but the user experience falls short on mobile viewports due to a bulky sticky header, and average lottery players may find the technical English jargon intimidating.

#### What's Working
1. **Color Strategy Implementation:** The Restrained gold accent (≤10%) in Thai mode and the purple switch in Lao mode are beautifully executed. Changing modes successfully shifts the ambient color system.
2. **Solid Form Controls & Prevention:** Inputs are robustly validated and the inline Lock overlay on the History tab for guest users gracefully restricts access without annoying popups.
3. **Digit Balls Visuals:** The temperature-coded radial gradients (Hot, Cold, Overdue) look fantastic and match the conceptual direction.

#### Priority Issues

- **[P1] Inconsistent Focus Outline Colors**
  - **Why it matters:** Focus rings use gold (`var(--gold)`) even in Lao mode, violating the Mode Signal Rule and leaking the wrong brand accent.
  - **Fix:** Update `.tab:focus-visible`, `.btn:focus-visible`, `.inp:focus-visible`, and other interactive outlines in `theme.css` to use `var(--accent)` or `var(--accent2)`.
  - **Suggested command:** `impeccable layout`

- **[P1] Identical Card Grids**
  - **Why it matters:** Grid sections look repetitive and lean into the SaaS cliché layout.
  - **Fix:** Vary layout widths, highlight the most critical cards (like "Hot Digits" or "Top Combos") with colored borders, distinct backgrounds, or specific icons.
  - **Suggested command:** `impeccable layout`

- **[P2] Mobile Sticky Header Height**
  - **Why it matters:** Stacking rows make the header up to 180px tall on mobile. Since it is sticky, it blocks nearly a third of the viewport.
  - **Fix:** Turn off header stickiness on mobile, or collapse the prize pills into a compact swipable row.
  - **Suggested command:** `impeccable adapt`

- **[P2] Technical Copy Jargon**
  - **Why it matters:** Jargon like "Advanced Hyperparameter Tuner" or "Markov Chain Weight" causes cognitive overload for average players.
  - **Fix:** Translate and simplify English labels into Thai: e.g., "Advanced Hyperparameter Tuner" -> "ปรับแต่งค่าน้ำหนักสถิติ (Tuner)", "Historical Backtesting Simulator" -> "ระบบจำลองผลย้อนหลัง (Backtesting)".
  - **Suggested command:** `impeccable clarify`

- **[P3] Banned Em-Dashes**
  - **Why it matters:** Violates the absolute ban on em-dashes (`—`) in text copy.
  - **Fix:** Replace `—` in all component text with colons or parentheses.
  - **Suggested command:** `impeccable typeset`

#### Persona Red Flags

* **Jordan (First-Timer):** Jordan feels overwhelmed by the AI Predict tab. There is no simple description explaining what "Markov Chain" or "Z-Score" means. The lack of tooltips or contextual help makes him distrust the calculations, leading to high bounce rates.
* **Alex (Power User):** Alex tweaks the weight ratios but finds it annoying to manually slide them back to defaults because there is no "Reset" button.
* **ป้าสมศรี (Senior Mobile User):** She opens the site on her phone at night. The header is too tall, and she struggles to read the Front 3 digits in the header pill because the font size is only 13px.

#### Minor Observations
- No visual loader for historical charts, causing slight layout shifting during backtesting calculations.
- Hardcoded red color (`#ef5350`) for the logout button has sub-optimal contrast in light mode.

#### Questions to Consider
- What if the AI Predict tab had an "Explain this math" toggle for non-technical users?
- Could we add a "Reset to Defaults" button on the Hyperparameter Tuner to increase flexibility?
- Should we hide the header prize pills on mobile viewports to reclaim screen real estate?
